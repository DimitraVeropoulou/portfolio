About

Research-driven, code-first, and focused on useful spatial evidence.

I am an MSc Geo-Information Sciences student working on agricultural remote sensing, with a strong focus on turning satellite imagery into practical insights for crop monitoring, harvest prediction, and decision support.

My work sits at the intersection of geospatial science, agronomy, and data analysis. I work comfortably across R, Python, QGIS, and Google Earth Engine, and I care deeply about workflows that are robust, reproducible, and interpretable.

Most of my recent projects center on sugarcane systems in Sub-Saharan Africa, where I use Sentinel-2 imagery, cloud masking, vegetation indices, and machine learning to understand crop condition and support harvest planning.

Beyond research, I enjoy building clean data pipelines, documenting methods clearly, and translating technical outputs into visuals and summaries that collaborators can actually use.
